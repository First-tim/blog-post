Organic foods are grown in a way that protects the environment, conserves natural resources, and produces safe, healthy food. Organic foods are often more nutritious than conventionally grown foods. Studies have shown that organic foods contain higher levels of antioxidants and essential vitamins and minerals. Additionally, organic foods are often fresher because they are not treated with preservatives. Organic foods are also safer to eat than conventionally grown foods. Many conventional farming practices involve the use of toxic chemicals that can be harmful to human health. Eating organic reduces your exposure to these chemicals and helps to protect your health. Organic foods are also better for the environment. Organic farming practices are designed to conserve natural resources and reduce pollution. Organic farming does not use synthetic fertilizers, which can contaminate groundwater and harm wildlife. Organic farming also uses fewer resources and produces less waste.
